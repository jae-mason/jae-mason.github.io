Ko Wai Au? (Who am I?)

Kia ora koutou, Ko Jae tooku ingoa, No Tamaki-Makaurau ahau, Kei Henderson ahau e noho ana. Hello all, My name is Jae and I am from Auckland. I am currently living in Henderson. At the moment I am on a break from a 14 year career in the insurance industry and thought now is a great time or a career change and to do something I had a passion for prior to starting a family 19 years ago. I place a lot of value on my family as they mean the most to me - instilling the key values of whanaungatanga, kotahitanga, aroha, rangatiratanga and manaakitanga have been a huge part of my life until this point and will continue to be in the future.

My personality types, core personal values, strengths and limitations.

Based on the Myers Briggs and DOPE testing I am described in the following way.

  • I = Introvert
  • S = Sensing
  • T = Thinking
  • P = Perceiving

I am a logical, quietly analytical, practical, adaptable, curious, cool, observer, problem solver, exact, realistic, troubleshooter, hands-on, variety, adventurous and independent.

  • Owl =45%
  • Dove = 35%
  • Eagle = 15%
  • Peacock = 5%

I am a logical, mathematically minded, methodical, perfectionist that loves details. However, I have low assertiveness and low emotionality.

Others see me as dedicated, committed and persistent with a great sense of humour.

There are two core personal values that are fundamental to me as an individual - integrity, and honesty.

There was a time when I identified fraudulent transactions in my workspace. To my surprise they were undertaken by someone I considered a friend. My ethics were on the line. I had options to weigh up. Integrity and honesty or deceit and lies, this was a substantial theft.

Thinking personally about my cultural Maori values, and Tikanga, to always do things right, I believe these have made me a better person and helped me to make the right choices. I had to report the fraud and request a full investigation into the theft. At the end of the day, it was not my burden to carry.

My strengths really help with my personal learning development, especially when it's in an independent hands-on environment. I like to expand my learning experience and stretch the topics the best I can.

Career Development for me within the insurance claims profession was varied. My personal values always had me expanding my knowledge and pushing myself to become the best I could be. This resulted in me being a well respected, knowledgable and approachable team member. I do believe my lack of self-confidence and group speaking deficiencies have prevented me from further progressing my career into management.

I believe one of my greatest strengths is being a problem solver, I should be able to find ways to overcome barriers along the way to becoming a great developer.
